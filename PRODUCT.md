# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Kumaya Burning Man camp leads, committee members, and Burnie, the AI planning agent, use this surface while coordinating camp operations. Users are checking what needs attention, who owns work, what changed, and which source-backed planning records need review.

## Product Purpose

The product is an internal planning hub for Kumaya. It renders source-backed YAML planning records into an attention-first dashboard for tasks, areas, meetings, decisions, timeline milestones, and changelog updates. Success means camp leads can quickly orient, trust the provenance of information, and decide what to do next without turning the site into a generic project-management clone or an in-browser editing system.

## Brand Personality

Warm, operational, trustworthy. The interface should feel camp-specific and human enough for a Burning Man team, but disciplined enough that leads trust it during logistics work.

## Anti-references

Do not make it look like a generic SaaS project-management clone, a Jira/Asana imitation, a decorative Burning Man poster, or an overproduced marketing landing page. Avoid gratuitous hero sections, noisy decorative cards, heavy editing affordances, and anything that implies the website is the source of truth instead of the rendered view of source-backed planning data.

## Design Principles

1. Attention first: the most urgent tasks, dates, updates, and review needs should surface before archival detail.
2. Source-backed trust: provenance and confidence should be visible enough to support decisions without dominating every card.
3. Operational calm: dense planning information should stay readable, scannable, and predictable under time pressure.
4. Camp specificity without costume: use Kumaya/Burning Man character through palette, type, and naming, not decorative gimmicks.
5. AI-maintained clarity: make it clear that Burnie maintains structured data while humans use the site to orient and act.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard accessibility. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Do not rely on color alone for status or urgency. Keep source/provenance controls labeled and reachable for screen-reader and keyboard-only users.
