#### Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: This does not look like a generic AI landing page. It has real data, a strong camp-specific palette, and a useful read-only operating model. The AI tell is subtler: repeated card/table/list grammar, lots of similarly weighted badges, and a long overview that feels generated from data structures rather than shaped around a human’s next decision. The design is competent, but not yet opinionated about attention.

**Deterministic scan**: CLI detector found 3 warnings in `app/globals.css`: Geist / Geist Mono overused-font findings at lines 1, 85, and 107. Browser overlay found recurring issues across pages: low contrast on red/cream text combinations, cramped padding around timeline rail containers, tiny 11px metadata text, long line lengths in meeting/update prose, nested cards, clipped overflow containers, and one skipped heading sequence from the Meetings section.

**Visual overlays**: Overlay injection succeeded in the browser. The console reported issues on the Tasks, Overview, and Timeline surfaces, including low-contrast badges, nested card structures, tiny text, long prose lines, and clipped overflow containers. Some nested-card findings are partly false positives because shadcn-style cards are being used as the dominant shell, but the signal matches the visual critique: the hierarchy is too dependent on repeated bordered containers.

#### Overall Impression

The site has the right raw material: trustworthy data, a distinctive oxblood/cream mood, and a clear internal-planning purpose. The biggest opportunity is to stop treating every record as equal. A camp lead should land here and instantly know: what changed, what is risky, what needs ownership, and what is due next.

#### What’s Working

1. **The dark oxblood visual world is memorable and appropriate.** It avoids generic SaaS blue/white and feels connected to Kumaya without becoming decorative Burning Man cosplay.
2. **The information architecture is sound.** Timeline, tasks, areas, meetings, decisions, and updates map well to how leads think about camp planning.
3. **Source/confidence is a strong product idea.** The UI already distinguishes AI-maintained, source-backed records from arbitrary content. That is strategically important and worth preserving.

#### Priority Issues

**[P1] The overview is a data dump, not an attention system**

- **Why it matters**: Camp leads are time-constrained. The overview shows many true things, but it does not make the next action obvious. Tasks, area cards, meeting excerpts, decisions, and update bullets all compete for attention.
- **Fix**: Add an explicit attention strip near the top: overdue/soon due, unowned high-priority work, needs-review count, and latest change. Then reduce lower sections to denser previews with clearer “why this matters” cues.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout /Users/twin/Context/burnie/web/app/page.tsx`

**[P1] Review/unowned risk is under-emphasized**

- **Why it matters**: The task page shows 28 to review and 16 unowned, which are operationally serious. But the table rows make those look like routine metadata, not risk. This weakens the dashboard’s ability to guide action.
- **Fix**: Turn review/unowned into triage affordances: default sort/group by risk, highlight unowned high-priority rows, add a compact “needs owner” view, and make review status more meaningful than a Yes/No pill.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable shape task triage view`

**[P1] Accessibility and hierarchy issues are real, not cosmetic**

- **Why it matters**: The detector found low contrast, tiny 11px text, skipped heading structure, and clipped overflow containers. These affect low-vision, keyboard, and screen-reader users, and they also make the interface feel less trustworthy.
- **Fix**: Raise contrast for red-on-dark and cream-on-red combinations, replace 11px body metadata with at least 12-13px where it carries meaning, fix heading order in meeting previews, and audit focus states on clickable rows and source links.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable audit http://127.0.0.1:8080`

**[P2] The card/list grammar is too repetitive**

- **Why it matters**: Areas, milestones, meetings, decisions, and updates often share the same bordered-card cadence. The page becomes visually consistent, but monotonous. It is hard to tell which content is a summary, a record, an action, or a source-backed citation.
- **Fix**: Assign distinct presentation modes: tasks as table/triage, areas as compact ownership tiles, decisions as borderless durable calls, updates as changelog entries, meetings as minutes excerpts. Reduce nested border shells.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable distill overview sections`

**[P2] Timeline precision overwhelms timeline meaning**

- **Why it matters**: Daily grid lines and multiple milestone representations communicate precision, but the important story is simpler: fundraising, dues, pre-build, gates, burn week. Current chart detail risks becoming texture instead of information.
- **Fix**: Keep daily lines subtle or only on hover/zoom, emphasize phase bands and upcoming deadlines, and collapse duplicate upcoming list/card representations.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout timeline`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Alex, Power User**

- Wants to triage quickly, but must visually scan 32 task rows because risk is not grouped into an owner/review/due workflow.
- Sorting exists, but no saved views like “Unowned high priority,” “Needs review,” or “Due soon.”
- Row click affordance is weak: rows are clickable, but the visual target is just the task title/button inside a dense row.

**Sam, Accessibility-Dependent User**

- Detector found low contrast at 3.6:1 and 4.4:1, below WCAG AA for normal text.
- Heading order is imperfect on the overview Meetings section, with an h2 followed by h4.
- Tiny metadata and hover-only timeline labels are not enough for users who cannot rely on hover or fine visual discrimination.

**Jordan, First-Timer**

- The site explains that it is source-backed, but not what confidence means, what “needs review” requires, or how Burnie changes records.
- “28 to review” and “16 unowned” look important, but the next step is not named.
- The overview has many sections, so a first-timer understands the data model faster than the workflow.

#### Minor Observations

- The nav pill is polished and consistent, but the theme toggle is icon-only and could use a stronger accessible label/tooltip in visible UI.
- The area grid works, but ten similarly sized area cards create a scanning burden. Lead and task count should be easier to compare.
- The updates page has very long changelog prose lines; cap measure and chunk changed objects more aggressively.
- The Spectral headings add warmth. Geist body is acceptable for product UI despite the detector warning, but the site could use either a more distinctive body face or stronger typographic roles to avoid feeling templated.
- The timeline currently presents both chart markers and list cards for the same events; that duplication should earn its keep or be simplified.

#### Questions to Consider

- What should a camp lead do in the first 30 seconds after opening the site: assign owners, review AI-inferred records, check upcoming deadlines, or read what changed?
- Should “needs review” mean a formal workflow state with ownership, or just a warning badge?
- Does the overview need to show every planning domain, or only domains with current risk/change?
- What would make the source/confidence model feel reassuring instead of bureaucratic?
